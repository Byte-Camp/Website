Camp Information

What camps and programs do Byte Camp offer?

Do I need to supply my own laptop/tablet?

Will food be provided?

Can I sign up for a camp that I'm too young for?

Why do we spend so much time outside?

How come we dont face the front during camp?

If you jump over to our Programs > Camps [//insert link] page, you will find that we have provided a comprehensive guide on every Byte Camp program we are actively (and can offer in the future) running with details on camp goals and catered age group.
Byte Camp will provide all the necessary technical equipment for student use during the entirety of the program so no need to supply your own devices.
Meals and snacks are not provided at Byte Camp. Week long camp participants are expected to bring their own bagged lunch and snacks, and after school participants are encouraged to bring snacks to consume prior to the start of their class.
After all these years of experience with working with children and teaching creative technology, we find that the 9-12 age range and 11-14 age range works best with the type of content we are presenting. As such, our camps have been carefully designed and piloted with particular age groups in mind. We do not wish for any camper to be discouraged or ‘turned off’ by creative tech because they are being introduced earlier than they should be.Parents are welcomed (and encouraged!) to contact a regional manager to discuss if their child is ready for the camp, or for recommendations if they’re not quite ready.
We are not a school room and we want to keep it more of a fun camp environment than a classroom. We find that taking the children outside for some organized play time really helps reboot their brains and refreshes their creative flow before taking on the next challenge. On top of health concerns such as eye exhaustion and muscle aches, the fresh air and leg stretching really does benefit the children both physically and mentally.
With so many cool things we want to teach the participants in our limited time span with them, for distracted minds to stray off because they want to play with the laptops and/or mice, they will miss out on a lot of awesome information. By having the students turn around and focus for short bursts of lessons, the students are less distracted by the devices and more likely to retain what was taught.

The Organization

What is Byte Camp?

Why is Byte Camp the best tech camp for kids?

Where are we located?

Why are the camps so inexpensive/expensive?

Byte Camp is a non-profit organization specializing in creative tech programming for kids. With ever evolving technology, it is our goal to demonstrate to the kids that there is more to laptops, tablets and phones than just consuming and using.
As one of Western Canada’s most busiest technological program providers, Byte Camp is not just another computer camp. We do not simply just teach how to use programs on electronic devices. It is our hope that with every participant we can encourage them to explore creatively and experience opportunities in creative tech they wouldn’t be able to anywhere else, by focusing more on the why instead of the what of technology. Since operations began in 2003, we have been continually growing and expanding, and we are always exploring new opportunities to introduce and share new ways of creatively working with technology.
Byte Camp is located in various locations all over Victoria, Metro Vancouver, and Calgary! Simply hop over to our calendar [//insert link] page to view all currently active location. Activities and locations change seasonally!
Byte Camp is a not-for-profit organization. As such, the prices we charge are set just low enough for us to cover our facilitation fees and resource costs. Because of facilitation fees, our fees may vary from location to location. Our wish is for our programming to be available to as many children as possible and so, we try to set our fees as low as we can.
